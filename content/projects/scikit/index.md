Artificial intelligence-enabled solutions are emerging
as powerful tools for handling power systems’ complex, high-
dimensional problems. However, these methods are inherently
data-intensive to generate accurate operational solutions. These
requirements put Sub-Saharan Africa (SSA) utilities in a tough
spot as to how to take advantage of these advances to strengthen
their weak utilities, as the majority still rely on analog technology.
In this study, we perform the empirical assessment of the rela-
tionship between data availability and utility performance using
statistical modeling. We found that six out of seven operational
indicators exceed a 50 percent data absence, with transmission-
level indicators missing in 87 percent across country-years. Our
findings demonstrate that the data gap and the performance
gap in SSA utilities are not independent problems but a data
infrastructural deficit. We conclude with a roadmap identifying
the data structures and imputation strategies, providing a prac-
tical starting point for practitioners and researchers seeking to
advance AI adoption in African power systems.
